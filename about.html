About Skywalker's Surplus

Some things you stumble into. This was not one of them.

At Derby Park flea market in Louisville, there was a booth that had been there for 27 years. The last 19 belonged to one operator known around here as Colonel Clink. CB gear, ham equipment, bench tools, power supplies, variacs, meters, and anything else electronic he thought he could nurse back to life. Floor to ceiling. Wall to wall. You could not get into it.

When the opportunity came to take it all, I took it without a second thought. Every radio. Every meter. Every mystery box in the back corner. Over 250 radios and still counting, because I am not even done moving it all home yet. I bought five extra weeks of booth rental just to clear it out. Two and a half weeks left and there is still plenty more to go.

I am SkyWalker. I run a base station here in Louisville, chase skip on 38 LSB, and have spent years building something I genuinely care about. One antenna, one repair, one contact at a time. That is not just how I approach radio. It is how I approach everything.

When I put a radio on the bench and bring it back, it is not just a transaction. That radio sat on somebody's desk. It was the voice somebody's family heard coming through the speaker on a long haul. It was the rig somebody saved up for. It carried real conversations and real moments that nobody wrote down anywhere. When it powers back up and the needle moves and the audio comes through clean, that matters to me on a level that is hard to explain to someone who has never felt it.

Every piece that comes out of this collection gets that same attention. Opened up, inspected, powered up on the bench, and described honestly. If it works, I will tell you how well. If it needs something, I will tell you that too. Or I will fix it first.

This is not a liquidation. It is a continuation.

Colonel Clink spent 27 years pulling in gear that deserved another shot. I walked into that booth, looked at 19 years of history stacked floor to ceiling, and felt the full weight of it. I still do some days. But I knew exactly what it was. And I knew exactly what it deserved.

One radio at a time. Every one of them gets to live again.
